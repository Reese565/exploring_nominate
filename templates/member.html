Congressional Members Ideological Drift

Do People Ever Change?

It remains an open question whether congressional members, or individuals more broadly, ever undergo substantial ideological change. Moreover, have congressional members noticeably changed during their times in office? Nokken and Poole created a socioeconomic ideological dimension which assumes that each congress is independent from all others for the purpose of measuring each memberâ€™s ideology. Taking advantage of this, we have developed a tool which allows users to summarize how individual congressional members have evolved ideologically during their times in congress.
