On Political Polarization in the United States

Introduction

This project aims to present the findings of research in political polarization in the United State Congress. The project will provide an easy source for those with a casual interest to consume the data along with some key research findings. Additionally, researchers looking to explore patterns in polarization may find utility in this tool to possibly identify patterns that may help them generate new research hypotheses in the areas of history and political science.

Data

Our data are every congressional roll call vote in American history on a map of the United States and on a liberal-conservative ideological map including information about the ideological positions of voting Senators and Representatives. The bipolar ideological scoring system was originally developed by Keith T. Poole and Howard Rosenthal at Carnegie-Mellon University. Metadata on each congressperson is also provided including party affiliation, district, state, as well as other attributes that may be of interest to the project. The data are publicly available and free to download here.
